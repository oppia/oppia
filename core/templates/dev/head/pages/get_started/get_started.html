Get Started!

Creating an exploration is easy and free. Share your knowledge with students around the world, and get feedback you can use to improve your exploration's effectiveness.

Choose a Topic

All you need to get started is a topic you want to teach. You can create an exploration about any topic, large or small. The ideal size of topic for an exploration is one that you would cover in a single class. You can also create multiple related explorations that are intended to be completed in sequence. This is called a collection.

Create Your Exploration

When you have chosen a topic, just click ‘Create’, and log in with your Google account. If you don't have a Google account, you can create one here.

An exploration consists of multiple steps. Each step can can include text (e.g. a written explanation), pictures and video. Each step presents the student with a question, which they must attempt in order to continue. It can be a multiple choice question, require them to type something in, or be any of a number of other available interactions.

Once the learner has answered the question, Oppia will give them feedback and let them continue to the next step. To see how learners experience Oppia, try out one of these explorations:

  • Welcome to Oppia
  • Introduction to Chili Peppers
  • Pitch Perfect

More information about creating explorations can be found here.

Publish Your Exploration

Once you've created your exploration and you're ready for learners to see it, click the 'Publish' button at the top of the page. This will make your exploration available to learners around the world!

Share Your Exploration

After you publish the exploration, you can share it via a link, or even embed it in your own web page.

Improve Your Exploration

When learners go through your exploration, they can send you feedback to alert you to problems or to share ideas for making it better.

In addition, as learners use your exploration, you'll be able to see the common errors they're making. Sometimes this can reveal new insights about where they are confused. It's easy to augment the exploration with additional feedback if you think that other learners are likely to repeat these errors. You could even send the learner to a different step, or "go deeper" by asking another question.

Get Involved

To get involved in the Oppia project and help us bring about our mission of free, high-quality universal education, contact us at admin@oppia.org, or discover more ways to get involved with our community of volunteers. We're excited to hear from you!
