Get Started

Creating an Oppia lesson is easy and free. After you create the lesson, you share it with others, and get feedback you can use to improve the lesson.

Choose a Topic

All you need to get started is a topic you want to teach. You can create a lesson about any topic, large or small. The ideal size of topic for a lesson is one that you would cover in a single class. You can also create multiple related lessons that are intended to be completed in sequence.

Create Your Lesson

When you have chosen a topic, just click ‘Create’, and log in with your Google account. If you don't have a Google account, you can create one here.

A lesson consists of a number of steps. A step can can include text (e.g. a written explanation), pictures and video. Each step presents the student with a question, which they must attempt in order to continue. It can be a multiple choice question, or require them to type something in, or any one of a number of other available interactions.

Once the learner has answered the question, Oppia will give them feedback and let them continue to the next step. To see how learners experience Oppia, try out one of these lessons:

  • Welcome to Oppia
  • Introduction to Chili Peppers
  • Pitch Perfect

More information about creating lessons can be found here.

Publish Your Lesson

Once you've created your lesson and you're ready for learners to see it, click the 'Publish' button at the top of the page. This will make your lesson available to learners around the world!

Share Your Lesson

After you publish the lesson, you can share it via a link, or even embed it in your own web page.

Improve Your Lesson

When learners go through your lesson, they can send you feedback to alert you to problems or to share ideas for making it better.

In addition, as learners use your lesson, you'll be able to see the common errors they're making. Sometimes this can reveal new insights about where they are confused. It's easy to augment the lesson with additional feedback if you think that other learners are likely to repeat these errors. If you wanted to, you could even send the learner to a different step, or "go deeper" by asking another question.

Get Involved

To get involved in the Oppia project and help us bring about our mission of free, high-quality universal education, reach out via our oppia-dev@ Google Group, or contact us at admin@oppia.org. We're excited to hear from you!

Also, if you have any feedback for us, please don't hesitate to let us know via our feedback form — we read all the feedback we receive. Thanks in advance!
