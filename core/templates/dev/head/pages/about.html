Oppia "O-pee-yah" (Finnish) - "to learn"

Oppia's mission: To help anyone learn anything they want in an effective and enjoyable way.

How do pendulums work? Is light a particle or a wave? What is art? Can you explain topics like these to a student who wants to learn more? If so, you can use Oppia's unique technology to recreate 1-on-1 tutoring and reach students around the world by creating and sharing a single piece of learning material.

With Oppia, you can create explorations that help students learn in a fun and creative way, using videos, images, and open-ended questions. Students often have similar misconceptions, and Oppia helps you organize these so that you can give targeted feedback to a group of students at a time.

By teaching with Oppia, you can communicate with students around the world, understand different points of view, and improve your skills in communication and empathy. Best of all, the services we provide are free, online, and fully accessible to learners and teachers all over the world. We also run a Teach with Oppia program whose aim is to provide lessons for topics that students typically find difficult.

Don't feel comfortable enough to teach yet? You can also collaborate with other Oppia creators by sharing feedback on lessons to help create a better experience for students.

Whether you're a K-12 educator, a graduate student, or an individual who's passionate about a specific subject and wants to share your knowledge, Oppia welcomes you. Join the community and start exploring with us.

The Oppia Foundation

The Oppia website and source code are supported by the Oppia Foundation, a tax-exempt 501(c)(3) non-profit organization registered in the State of California.

The directors of the Foundation are Amit Deutsch, Jacob Davis, and Sean Lip. The Foundation's bylaws and minutes are available to read. If you wish to contact the Foundation, please email: admin@oppia.org.
